About Me

My name is Hazel Apuhin. I've always been dreaming of getting paid while I travel before the internet was already "a thing". Maybe the itch to travel came from my late grandpa Pedro, so I've been told but never met, whom traveled the world and had an exciting life. His lust for travel brought him the idea to cross the Pacific Ocean in 1967 to sail from the Philippines to the United States with his hand-built 40 x 4 feet wooden outrigger and calls the trip "maritime honor"! I know, how crazy is that right!? Well, he failed after being battered by a typhoon and found themselves in Taiwan. But still, I find it so awesome and cool.

I took up Electronics and Communications Engineering in TUP and La Salle Bacolod. But, unfortunately did not finish a degree. So I work my way up from working as a service crew in a fast food chain, then became a custodian in an amusement center, joined the BPO industry and became sales, customer service, technical support, IT help desk and now to online freelancing. I now can make 6-figure monthly salary but only if I juggle 3 jobs. With the money I have, I started my investment portfolio, invested in real estate, I traveled to 17 countries in just a little over 2 years. Gained a lot of friends from all walks of life everywhere I go. And now, I am working on building my digital assets. For over 2 years, I learnt how my clients do their business. I think both my offline and online experiences honed me enough to start my own online business too. And so I did.

Being a start up is difficult but I strive everyday to learn something useful to my business and thats why I learned coding and pretty much enjoying it

I never knew or planned the life I have or will be having in the future. I only know this, I never get too comfortable with where I am at now. I continue to strive and grow everyday. Be open to new ideas and always try new things.  I don't bind myself with fears and insecurities coz it will eat me up and hold me back to what I might become. Looking back, I think I've come far from where I was 10 years ago. And never stopped to better myself, one day at a time.
